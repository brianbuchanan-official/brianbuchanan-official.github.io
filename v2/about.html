About

I am a recent Actuarial Science graduate from Baruch College, having begun my academic journey at Queensborough Community College. My background is rooted in quantitative problem-solving and a drive to understand the “why” behind the numbers. I thrive at the intersection of data-driven strategy and real-world application, always looking for ways to use my analytical training to solve complex business challenges.

On the technical side, I focus on bridging the gap between raw data and strategic decision-making. To accelerate my professional growth, I challenged myself to master the financial regulatory landscape, successfully passing the FINRA Securities Industry Essentials (SIE) and the New York Insurance exam within the same two-week window. I am currently licensed in New York, New Jersey, and Florida. My technical toolkit includes Python, SQL, Power BI, and Advanced Excel, and I leverage AI-driven workflows to improve productivity, audit code for accuracy, and build custom digital tools and websites efficiently.

I have been a dedicated Lifeguard since 2016, supported by a lifelong foundation in competitive swimming that began at age eight. As a certified YMCA Swim Instructor and Lifeguard, I have spent years assisting coaches and mentoring swimmers in developing life-saving techniques. My goal is to help individuals move from discomfort around water to confidence, reinforcing the idea that growth comes from challenge in a safe, supportive environment.

This same commitment to helping others grow extends to my work as a pro-bono Algebra Tutor. By offering free support to students struggling with mathematical concepts, I have strengthened my ability to translate complex, technical information into clear, accessible explanations. Whether in the pool or the classroom, I am motivated by the opportunity to make a positive impact and help others achieve their goals.
