About Me

I’m an Actuarial Science graduate from Baruch College, and I began my academic journey at Queensborough Community College. My background is rooted in quantitative problem solving and a strong curiosity for understanding the why behind numbers. I enjoy work at the intersection of data driven strategy and real world decision making, where analytical insight supports business outcomes.

On the technical side, I focus on translating raw data into actionable insights. I challenged myself to master the financial regulatory landscape by passing the FINRA Securities Industry Essentials exam and the New York Insurance exam within the same two week period. I am currently licensed in New York, New Jersey, and Florida. My toolkit includes Python, SQL, Power BI, and Advanced Excel, and I apply these tools across portfolio projects focused on finance, risk, and performance analysis.

Across my academic training and professional experience, I’ve developed a strong understanding of financial products, pricing, and risk, particularly within insurance and long term financial planning. I learn quickly, adapt easily, and perform best in environments that value analytical depth, clear communication, and thoughtful decision making.

Outside of analytics, I have been a dedicated Lifeguard since 2016 and a certified YMCA Swim Instructor. These roles reinforced accountability, attention to detail, and leadership under pressure, qualities that translate well to professional settings where trust and precision matter.
