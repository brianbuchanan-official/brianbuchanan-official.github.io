About Me

I’m an Actuarial Science graduate from Baruch College, and I began my academic journey at Queensborough Community College. My background is rooted in quantitative problem-solving and a drive to understand the “why” behind the numbers. I enjoy work at the intersection of data-driven strategy and real-world decision-making.

On the technical side, I focus on bridging the gap between raw data and business outcomes. I challenged myself to master the financial regulatory landscape by passing the FINRA Securities Industry Essentials (SIE) and the New York Insurance exam within the same two-week window, and I’m currently licensed in New York, New Jersey, and Florida. My toolkit includes Python, SQL, Power BI, and Advanced Excel, and I use AI-assisted workflows to improve productivity and catch errors early.

Across my academic training and portfolio projects, I’ve developed an understanding of financial products, pricing, and risk — including insurance and annuities. I learn quickly, adapt easily, and enjoy roles that require analytical depth and business judgment, with a focus on turning complex information into clear, actionable insights.

Outside of analytics, I’ve been a dedicated Lifeguard since 2016 and a certified YMCA Swim Instructor. I value environments that prioritize accountability, safety, and growth — and I bring that same mindset into professional work.
